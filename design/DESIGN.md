# Design System Documentation: The Executive Brief

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: Editorial Formality in Light**

This design system moves the portfolio away from dark, blue-heavy technical aesthetics and into a calmer, more executive mode. The site should feel professional, aesthetic, formal, and premium without becoming cold or generic. The visual language is editorial rather than dashboard-like: generous spacing, quiet asymmetry, strong serif hierarchy, and refined gradient atmosphere.

The first viewport must read as a composed poster, not as a template. Business continuity, information security, and business analysis lead the narrative. Machine learning remains visible as technical depth, but it should never overpower the present-day professional identity.

## 2. Colors
The foundation is light mode only.

- Base surfaces: warm ivory, pearl, soft stone, and mist
- Accent family: taupe, mineral sage, and muted bronze
- Mood: bright, restrained, and layered rather than glossy or neon

Rules:
- No deep navy hero backgrounds
- No dominant cyan or electric blue surfaces
- Use gradients softly to create atmosphere, not spectacle
- Keep borders faint and low-contrast so hierarchy comes from spacing and tone first

## 3. Typography
Typography should carry the majority of the premium feel.

- **Display & Headings:** `Newsreader`
  Use large editorial scale, tight tracking, and occasional italic emphasis.
- **Body & UI:** `Manrope`
  Keep body text highly legible, quiet, and slightly condensed in feel.

Hierarchy targets:
- H1: roughly `56px` to `72px` on desktop and `36px` to `44px` on mobile
- Section headings: roughly `28px` to `36px`
- Body copy: roughly `17px` to `19px`
- Labels: small uppercase with generous tracking

## 4. Layout
The site remains one page, but the rhythm is intentionally editorial rather than strictly modular.

- The hero is a full composition with type on one side and a restrained systems visual on the other
- About is treated as an editorial spread, not a biography card
- Experience uses a refined vertical timeline
- Capabilities are grouped into disciplined bands instead of a noisy icon wall
- Projects use spotlight panels with light technical artwork
- Education, tools, and contact stay compact and factual

## 5. Components
### Buttons
- Primary: dark stone fill with light text
- Secondary: light surface with faint outline
- Shape: rounded, but not playful or pill-heavy

### Cards & Panels
- Default toward cardless layouts
- Use panels only when grouping materially improves scanning
- Panel shadows should be soft and warm, not dramatic

### Signature Visual
- A light continuity field with faint rings, contour lines, and connected nodes
- The visual should suggest resilience and systems thinking without feeling sci-fi

## 6. Do's and Don'ts
### Do
- Keep the page unmistakably light mode
- Use gradients in the hero and transitions between sections
- Let spacing and typography create authority
- Preserve truthful resume-backed copy

### Don't
- No dark theme sections
- No cyber or dashboard visuals
- No heavy stat strips or hero cards
- No invented copy or inflated claims
